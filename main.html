Project Overview

The "Jamais Vu" project aims to evoke the sensation of unfamiliarity by creating a synesthetic installation featuring a soundscape composed of crowdsourced audio samples from across Montreal. The project consists of two main components: 1 A database of sounds recorded by participants through a web app or a physical device, and 2 A generative soundscape installation accompanied by visual projections and suspended fabric sheets.

Our project uses "defamiliarization"—a technique termed by Viktor Shklovsky—to prompt new awareness of the familiar, subverting the usual interpretation of our reality. The collected soundscape offers participants an opportunity to engage critically with their environment, using technology not for efficiency but to create a sensory experience that challenges our perception of the world.
